What a Language Model Actually Is

Watch it being built up one idea at a time. No maths, no jargon — just the single trick everything is made of.

Step 1 of 7
The big idea

At heart, it is a prediction engine

Thecatsatonthe ???  ➜
Given a run of words, it answers one question, over and over: "What is most likely to come next?"

That is the whole trick. Every astonishing thing these systems do grows out of this one ability — applied at enormous scale.

Step 1 · How it reads

It doesn't see words — it sees tokens

"automation"
↓ broken into chunks ↓
auto mat ion

Your text is first chopped into small pieces called tokens — sometimes whole words, sometimes fragments.

Definition · Token

A small chunk of text. The model thinks in tokens, not words — which is why it occasionally trips up on spelling or counting letters.

Step 2 · How it decides

It ranks every possible next token

It gives every candidate a probability, then usually picks the front-runner — here, "mat".

Step 3 · The loop

Then it does it again… and again

Themodelwritesonetokenatatime

Each new token becomes part of the input for the next guess. Loop fast enough and you get essays, code and conversation.

Definition · Context

The context window: what it can hold in mind

Context window recent text stays in view
"older text falls off the back ↘"

Measured in tokens. Whatever fits, the model can use. Whatever spills past the edge is, for that chat, forgotten.

Definition · Context window

The amount of text the model can hold at once — from roughly 8,000 tokens (~6,000 words) to over a million in modern systems.

Definition · Temperature

Temperature: the predictability dial

Low temperature
consistent & conservative — great for facts and summaries
High temperature
varied & creative — great for brainstorming and stories

In chat apps this is usually set for you and hidden; in business tools you can often turn the dial yourself.

In one breath

Sophisticated — and unreliable

🧠
Sophisticated enough to be useful
Predicting well demands a working grasp of grammar, context, tone and culture.
⚖️
Unreliable enough to need you
It produces what is likely, not what is true. Human judgement is part of the system.

Both halves matter. That tension is exactly why these tools are powerful — and why they always need a person in the loop.
