The Architecture You Are Actually Talking To

When you open a chatbot, you are not talking straight to the model. Let's meet the layer in between — and see what "memory" really is.

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — the part you chat with is the assistant. It sits between you and the language model and runs the conversation.

The key fact

The model itself is stateless

What's my name? →
🧠
🫥 "I have no idea — I was handed only this message."

The model forgets everything between requests. On its own it has no memory of anything you said before.

The trick

So the assistant keeps the history

My name is Asha. Nice to meet you, Asha! What's my name?
📋 The assistant remembers the whole thread for you

The conversation memory you experience lives in the assistant — not in the model.

Behind the curtain

What "remembering" really does

What the model actually receives My name is Asha. Nice to meet you, Asha! What's my name?
↻ Every new turn, the assistant silently re-sends all the earlier messages too.

Now the model can answer "Asha" — because the history was bundled in with the new question, fresh each time.

The catch

When the chat grows too long

Context window recent turn recent turn newest turn oldest turn ↘

Once the thread can't all fit, the oldest parts fall off the back. The model never "forgot" them — the assistant simply stopped including them.

Why it matters

Take it with you

💬
Assistant = memory
It manages history, safety rules, and tools.
🧠
Model = stateless
It only ever sees what it's handed, right now.
📏
Long chats drift
Start a fresh chat to bring lost context back.

If a long conversation seems to "lose the plot," now you know exactly why — and what to do about it.

✅ Practical tip: for important details, restate them — or begin a new chat — rather than assuming the model still has them.
